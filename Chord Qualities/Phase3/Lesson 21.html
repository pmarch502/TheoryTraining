Lesson 21: Half-Diminished Seventh

Much like the diminished triad in an earlier lesson, we now come to a seventh you won’t see very often. (But, when it’s used well, it’s so good…) The half-diminished seventh adds subtle tension in major keys, common in jazz or in worship modulations.

The actual half-diminished seventh formula is defined as:

  • Root
  • Minor third above root: an interval of 3 half steps, W+H. E.g., D to F.
  • Diminished fifth above root: an interval of 6 half steps, a minor third + a minor third, (W+H)+(W+H). E.g., B to F.
  • Minor seventh above root: an interval of 10 half steps, a major third + a minor third + a minor third, (W+W)+(W+H)+(W+H). (We might also call it a perfect fifth + a minor third.) E.g., C to B♭.

Again, this definition is very important because it is very precise - it has an important purpose in defining and naming chords.

Here is one example of a half-diminished seventh:

C half-diminished seventh: C - E♭ - G♭ - B♭

1   2   3   4   5   6   7   8
C   D   E   F   G   A   B   C
^       ♭       ♭       ♭

When a half-diminished seventh appears on a chord chart, it may appear with various chord quality decorations: Cø7, Cø, Cm7♭5, C-7♭5. The proper symbol may be the ‘ø7’, but that’s pretty hard to type on a normal keyboard. That’s where the ‘m7♭5’ comes in. Not only is it a completely accurate and unambiguous name, it can be typed as plain text (which has made it quite popular for chord charts).

When we talk about a half-diminished seventh according to scale degrees, we would call it a 1-♭3-♭5-♭7 (or, 1-3♭-5♭-7♭ - people say it both ways). (Remember - these numbers are scale degrees from the major scale of the root of the chord.)

Definitions

Diminished Fifth
Interval of six half steps (tritone), producing dissonance and tension (e.g., B to F).
Half-Diminished Seventh
Seventh chord with minor third, diminished fifth, and minor seventh (1-♭3-♭5-♭7), functioning as a pre-dominant or leading tone chord.
Key
Refers to the tonal center or scale upon which a musical composition is primarily based. It establishes the tonic (the central note) and defines the set of pitches, intervals, and chords that predominate in the piece, providing a sense of hierarchy, resolution, and harmonic framework.
Minor Seventh
Interval of ten half steps (perfect fifth + minor third), offering smooth, melancholic resolution (e.g., D to C).
Scale
An ordered sequence of notes, typically spanning an octave, arranged in ascending or descending order according to a specific pattern of intervals (whole steps and half steps, or tones and semitones). It serves as the foundational pitch collection for melodies, harmonies, and improvisation within a musical composition or tradition.
Seventh Chord
A four-note chord consisting of a root, third, fifth, and seventh, extending the triad with additional harmonic color and function.
