Lesson 20: Minor Seventh

Next is the minor seventh. The minor seventh sounds smooth, soulful—it’s common in worship for vi or ii chords, adding depth without tension.

The actual minor seventh formula is defined as:

  • Root
  • Minor third above root: an interval of 3 half steps, W+H. E.g., D to F.
  • Perfect fifth above root: an interval of 7 half steps, a major third + a minor third, (W+W)+(W+H). E.g., D to A.
  • Minor seventh above root: an interval of 10 half steps, a major third + a minor third + minor third, (W+W)+(W+H)+(W+H). (We might also call it a perfect fifth + a minor third.) E.g., C to B♭.

Again, this definition is very important because it is very precise - it has an important purpose in defining and naming chords.

Here is one example of a minor seventh:

C minor seventh: C - E♭ - G - B♭

1   2   3   4   5   6   7   8
C   D   E   F   G   A   B   C
^       ♭       ^       ♭

When a minor seventh appears on a chord chart, it may appear with various chord quality decorations: Cm7, Cmin7, Cmi7, C-7 (we have a minor triad (‘m’) and it’s not the 7 - it’s the ♭7 (no decoration)).

When we talk about a minor seventh according to scale degrees, we would call it a 1-♭3-5-♭7 (or, 1-3♭-5-7♭ - people say it both ways). (Remember - these numbers are scale degrees from the major scale of the root of the chord.)

Definitions

Key
Refers to the tonal center or scale upon which a musical composition is primarily based. It establishes the tonic (the central note) and defines the set of pitches, intervals, and chords that predominate in the piece, providing a sense of hierarchy, resolution, and harmonic framework.
Minor Seventh
Interval of ten half steps (perfect fifth + minor third), offering smooth, melancholic resolution (e.g., D to C).
Minor Third
Interval of three half steps (one whole and one half), producing a somber, narrow sound (e.g., A to C).
Perfect Fifth
Interval of seven half steps, providing stability and consonance (e.g., C to G).
Scale
An ordered sequence of notes, typically spanning an octave, arranged in ascending or descending order according to a specific pattern of intervals (whole steps and half steps, or tones and semitones). It serves as the foundational pitch collection for melodies, harmonies, and improvisation within a musical composition or tradition.
Seventh
An interval of ten or eleven half steps (minor or major seventh) above the root, adding tension, color, and extension to a triad when included in a seventh chord.
Triad
A three-note chord consisting of a root, third, and fifth, forming the basic building block of harmony.
